Vaikom Muhammad Basheer

writer, humanist, freedom , fighter, novelist

Early life

Born in Thalayolaparambu in Kottayam District as the eldest child of a timber businessman, Basheer even from his childhood days was attracted to Gandhian thoughts and ideologies. He participated in freedom struggles and was imprisoned which also inspired his writings. The cult classics of Basheer earned him a reputed position in the Indian Literature. Translations of his works have won worldwide acclaim.

legendary writer

Fondly known as Beypore Sultan, Basheer is an unparalleled genius in the selection of themes, characterisation and narration. Love, humanity, poverty and harsh realities of life all find their place in his works.

if there is one comfort in believing falsehood it is in making one feel equal to those who do know truths -Vaikom Muhammad Basheer

Awards

Sahitya Akademi

Sahitya Akademi honoured with the distinguished fellowship by the Kerala Sahitya award

Padma Shri

Padma Shri in 1982 and five years later, the University of Calicut conferred on him the honorary degree of the Doctor of Letters

Kerala State Film Award

Kerala State Film Award for Best Story for the Adoor Gopalakrishnan film, Mathilukal in 1989

Antharjanam Award

Lalithambika Antharjanam Award in 1992 followed by the Prem Nazir Award the same year
